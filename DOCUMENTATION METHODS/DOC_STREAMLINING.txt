1. FOUNDATION
======================================================================

Purpose:
Define the authoritative responsibility, interpretation,
applicability, and operational boundary of Document Streamlining.

Streamlining Contract Interpretation:
Every streamlining requirement expressed using "shall"
constitutes a mandatory documentation transformation constraint.

Streamlining behavior not explicitly required or permitted by
this Method or a higher-authority USER_PACKAGE document shall not
be assumed to be permitted.

Requirements owned by another authoritative USER_PACKAGE Method
or Standard shall not be interpreted as Document Streamlining
requirements.

Streamlining Definition:
Document Streamlining reduces unnecessary complexity in approved
documentation while preserving approved purpose, meaning,
behavior, functionality, and safety.

Applicability:
This Method applies only to approved documentation assets.

Excluded Assets:
- Source code.
- Runtime implementations.
- Executable assets.
- Behavioral Standards.
- Binary assets.
- Machine-readable serialization formats.

Responsibility Rule:
Document Streamlining governs documentation streamlining only.

======================================================================
3. STREAMLINING MODEL
======================================================================

Purpose:
Define the deterministic documentation streamlining model.

Streamlining Model:

Approved Documentation
        ↓
Complexity Analysis
        ↓
Redundancy Detection
        ↓
Meaning Preservation Check
        ↓
Minimum Safe Reduction
        ↓
Standards Compliance Review
        ↓
Streamlined Documentation

Model Interpretation:
Each stage shall complete before the next stage begins.

Model Boundary:
The Streamlining Model shall not approve new knowledge, redesign
architecture, or change document purpose.

======================================================================
4. STREAMLINING RULES
======================================================================

Do not change the document purpose.

Do not change the intended meaning.

Do not change operational behavior.

Do not remove required functionality.

Do not remove safety mechanisms.

Do not introduce new functionality unless
explicitly requested.

Only the minimum required changes shall be
applied.

Redundant statements shall be merged unless
doing so would alter approved meaning.

Unnecessary wording shall be removed without
changing approved meaning.

Preserve the original document structure unless
a structural improvement is clearly beneficial.

Streamlining Decision Rule:

Streamlining decisions shall be derived only
from:
- This Method.
- Applicable higher-authority USER_PACKAGE
  Standards.
- Explicit User instructions.
- Approved document meaning.

Streamlining decisions shall not be derived
from Assistant stylistic preference.

Streamlining Preservation Interpretation:

Preserving approved meaning, behavior,
functionality, and safety takes precedence over
reducing document size or improving readability.

Equivalent approved information shall produce
equivalent streamlined information unless
explicitly instructed otherwise.

Minimum Change Rule:
Only the minimum change required to remove validated unnecessary
complexity shall be applied.

Redundancy Rule:
Equivalent statements may be merged only when approved meaning
and applicability remain unchanged.

Structure Rule:
Existing valid structure shall be preserved unless a clearly
beneficial structural improvement remains within authorized
scope.

======================================================================
5. EXECUTION
======================================================================

Execute immediately when all required inputs
are available and no blocking issue exists.

Do not request execution confirmation.

Do not ask whether to proceed.

If no blocking issue exists,

begin streamlining immediately.

Blocking issues are the only valid reason
to suspend execution.

Execution resumes only after:
- the blocking issue has been resolved, or
- the user explicitly instructs otherwise.

Execution Sequence:
- Verify the approved baseline.
- Resolve authorized scope.
- Identify applicable Standards.
- Identify unnecessary complexity.
- Identify redundant statements.
- Verify preservation requirements.
- Apply the minimum safe reduction.
- Perform Standards Compliance Review.
- Correct validated compliance issues.
- Validate the completed streamlined document.

Execution Boundary:
Execution shall stop only when a validated blocker prevents safe
continuation.

======================================================================
6. CONFLICT HANDLING
======================================================================

If streamlining would:
- Change the document purpose.
- Change the intended meaning.
- Change operational behavior.
- Reduce safety.
- Remove required
  functionality.
- Introduce ambiguity.

Stop before modifying the document.

Report:
- Issue.
- Affected section(s).
- Reason.
- Recommended resolution.

Do not silently modify the document intent.

Do not modify the document until the conflict
has been resolved or the user explicitly
instructs otherwise.

Conflict Types:
- Purpose change.
- Meaning change.
- Operational behavior change.
- Functional behavior change.
- Safety reduction.
- Required functionality removal.
- Ambiguity introduction.
- Authority conflict.
- Standards conflict.

Conflict Prohibition:
Document Streamlining shall not silently change document intent,
meaning, behavior, functionality, or safety.

======================================================================
8. QUALITY CHECK
======================================================================

Before completing streamlining,

verify:
- Document purpose remains unchanged.
- Intended meaning remains unchanged.
- Operational behavior remains unchanged.
- Functional behavior remains unchanged.
- Safety remains unchanged.
- Readability has improved.
- Redundancy has been reduced.
- Document clarity has improved.
- No required information has been removed.
- No unsupported information has been introduced.

Before completing streamlining,

verify that the generated document complies
with all applicable Standards.

Complete Standards Compliance Review before
the Method is considered complete.

Correct identified Standards compliance issues
before completing the Method.

Standards Compliance Review applies only
to the generated streamlined document.

It shall not modify the approved purpose,
meaning, behavior, functionality, or safety
preserved by the streamlining process.

If no blocking issue exists,

immediately generate the final streamlined
document.

Otherwise, follow the Conflict Handling rules.

Streamlining Responsibility Boundary:

Document Streamlining shall not:
- Approve new knowledge.
- Redesign approved architecture.
- Modify approved responsibilities.
- Perform document refactoring.
- Perform document synthesis.
- Perform document consolidation.

Document Streamlining shall remain limited to
complexity reduction while preserving approved
meaning.

Validation Result:
- PASS: Every applicable streamlining requirement is satisfied.
- FAIL: One or more streamlining requirements are violated.
- NON-APPLICABLE: A requirement does not apply.

Failure Rule:
A streamlined document that fails Quality Check shall not proceed
to final delivery.

15. REVISION POLICY
======================================================================

Purpose:
Define when Document Streamlining may be revised.

Revision Criteria:
This Method may be revised only when validated streamlining work
demonstrates:

- Preservation ambiguity.
- Repeated meaning-change risk.
- Missing streamlining coverage.
- Redundancy-resolution failure.
- Safety-preservation failure.
- Standards compatibility failure.
- Responsibility overlap.
- Conflict with a higher-authority document.
- Proven maintenance cost.

Revision Rules:

Revision shall:
- Preserve the primary responsibility.
- Preserve approved purpose and meaning.
- Preserve approved behavior, functionality, and safety.
- Preserve compatibility when requirements remain valid.
- Resolve validated deficiencies before expanding scope.
- Preserve Single Authority Ownership.

Revision shall not:
- Absorb document refactoring.
- Absorb document synthesis.
- Absorb document consolidation.
- Absorb output rendering.
- Absorb Delivery Mode behavior.
- Introduce hidden assumptions.
- Authorize architecture redesign.
